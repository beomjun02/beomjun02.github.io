Papers

  • 🆕 FineVLA: Fine-Grained Instruction Alignment for Steerable Vision-Language-Action PoliciesarXiv cs.RO · 2026-05-26 · subtaskXintong Hu et al. argue that standard robot datasets pair trajectories with coarse goal-level language, omitting execution details like active arm, approach direction, and contact region. FineVLA contributes a human-verified fine-grained trajectory dataset, a held-out benchmark, a specialized VLM annotator, and a steerable policy — showing fine-grained + goal-level instructions are complementary for controlling pose, color, and approach.
  • 🆕 Can VLA Models Learn from Real-World Data Continually without Forgetting?arXiv cs.RO · 2026-05-26 · vlaJiarun Zhu et al. introduce a 4-task sequential manipulation benchmark (rigid pick-and-place, contact-rich pressing, deformable folding) and find that VLAs suffer substantial catastrophic forgetting on heterogeneous real demos. They evaluate experience replay and identify the implementation factors that actually decide whether it works — billed as the first empirical study of real-world continual VLA learning.
  • 🆕 Enabling Extensible Embodied Capabilities with ToolsarXiv cs.RO · 2026-05-26Xueyang Zhou et al. propose the Embodied Tool Protocol (ETP) — decoupling embodied capabilities into independently-optimized tools invoked at inference — with 100+ validated tools and an EmbodiedToolBench suite. Reports avg. gain of ~31% on EB-ALFRED and ~36% on EB-Navigation, with the caveat that perception/cognition gains outpace execution and tool-selection remains hard.
  • 🆕 Personalizing Embodied Multimodal Large Language Model Agents over Long-term User InteractionsarXiv cs.AI · 2026-05-25Jeongeun Lee et al. present POLAR, a memory-augmented embodied MLLM framework that organizes prior interactions into a multimodal knowledge graph with both semantic memory (personalized concepts) and episodic memory (agent trajectories). Across multiple MLLM backbones, the largest gains come from tasks requiring multi-hop inference over user-specific context.
  • 🆕 Exploiting Local Dynamics Regularity for Reusable Skills in Offline Hierarchical RLarXiv cs.AI · 2026-05-25Sarthak Dayal et al. argue that "local transitions in different global contexts require similar kinds of action sequences" and introduce CARL — Contrastive Action-based Representations for Reusable Local Control. Yields interpretable skill clustering on humanoid tasks and improves OGBench results when paired with HIQL.
  • 🆕 SkillOpt: Executive Strategy for Self-Evolving Agent SkillsarXiv / HF papers · 2026-05-24Yifan Yang et al. introduce a controllable text-space optimizer that treats a skill document as the trainable external state of a frozen agent: a separate optimizer model proposes bounded edits, accepted only when held-out validation strictly improves. Reports leading or tying across all 52 evaluated configurations spanning 6 benchmarks, 7 target models, and 3 execution harnesses; top HF Papers post of the week (175 upvotes).
