  • 🆕 Can VLA Models Learn from Real-World Data Continually without Forgetting?
    arXiv cs.RO · 2026-05-27 · VLABuilds a real-world continual-learning dataset across four sequential manipulation tasks (rigid pick-and-place, articulated, deformable, tool-use) and shows that standard VLA fine-tuning suffers severe catastrophic forgetting, while a simple replay+EWC variant largely recovers prior skills. Highlights how brittle current VLA deployment is once you leave the static finetune-and-freeze regime.
  • 🆕 FineVLA: Fine-Grained Instruction Alignment for Steerable Vision-Language-Action Policies
    arXiv cs.RO · 2026-05-27 · VLAIntroduces a data-construction pipeline that unifies 97 robotic datasets with execution-critical labels (active arm, approach direction, contact region) so that VLA policies can be steered by instruction modifiers rather than only goal text. The fine-grained supervision yields measurable gains in following "how" instructions without retraining the base policy.
  • 🆕 RoboMME: Benchmarking and Understanding Memory for Robotic Generalist Policies
    arXiv cs.RO · 2026-05-27 · VLAStanford/Michigan (Chelsea Finn, Joyce Chai) ship a large-scale standardized benchmark for long-horizon, history-dependent VLA tasks — counting repeated actions, manipulating objects under occlusion, etc. — and analyze how today's memory mechanisms (KV-cache vs. recurrent vs. retrieval) fare. Useful diagnostic for any group claiming "memory" in their generalist policy.
  • 🆕 WBench: A Comprehensive Multi-turn Benchmark for Interactive Video World Model Evaluation
    Hugging Face papers · 2026-05-27289 multi-turn test cases (1,058 interaction turns) scoring interactive video world models on quality, setting adherence, interaction adherence, temporal consistency, and physics compliance. Directly relevant to anyone building world-action models on top of video generators — finally a unified yardstick.
  • 🆕 Rethinking Cross-Layer Information Routing in Diffusion Transformers
    Hugging Face papers · 2026-05-27Systematic empirical study of how information flows along depth × denoising-timestep in DiTs, identifying three pathologies of the inherited residual stream and proposing a lightweight rewiring. Generation quality improves at matched compute on standard image/video DiT baselines — relevant to anyone training video diffusion backbones used downstream by world models.
  • 🆕 Quantized Keys Steal Attention: Bias Correction for KV-Cache Compression in Video Diffusion
    arXiv cs.LG · 2026-05-27 · video diffusion modelIdentifies a "Jensen bias" in softmax attention where quantization noise on cached keys systematically inflates their contribution, degrading long-video quality at low bitwidth. Their bias-correction term recovers most of the quality lost to 4-bit KV-cache compression in chunk-wise autoregressive video diffusion.
  • 🆕 SkillOpt: Executive Strategy for Self-Evolving Agent Skills
    Hugging Face papers · 2026-05-27Frames an agent's skill as the *external state* of a frozen base model and trains a separate optimizer model to apply bounded add/delete/replace edits driven by scored rollouts — the first principled text-space optimizer for skills. Top-upvoted paper on HF this week (↑177); the framing is interesting for anyone evolving generalist policies via post-hoc supervision.
  • 🆕 DVAO: Dynamic Variance-adaptive Advantage Optimization for Multi-reward RL
